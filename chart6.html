Conclusion

From the above, we can conclude that there are 2 factors that can explain the change of Lung/Bronchus Cancer Rate during the past 4 decades, Life Expectancy and Smoking.

There is a increase of life expectancy during the past 4 decades, which may contribute to the increase of Lung/Bronchus Cancer Rate.

However, the change of Smoking habit plays a even more important role. The odds of getting Lung/Bronchus Cancer among heavy smokers will be many times higher than non-smoker.
Also, as there is a delayed effect (around 20 years) between Smoking and Lung/Bronchus Cancer, we saw that there is around 20-30 years gap between the peak year of Smoking Rate and peak year of Lung/Bronchus Cancer.

So what kind of suggestions can we provide?

Needless to say, First:
NO Smoking!

Second:
For those over 40 years old, take regular health check, especially those over 65!
